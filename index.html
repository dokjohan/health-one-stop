🛡️ Is My Condition Covered? (PMB Check)
Show ▼

Prescribed Minimum Benefits (PMBs) are conditions every registered medical aid must cover by law — on every option, regardless of how basic the plan is. Check a condition below.

What the colours mean
Green — On the Chronic Disease List. Your scheme must cover this on any option.
Yellow — May be covered as a PMB in certain cases. Confirm with your scheme.
Red — Not a guaranteed PMB. Cover depends on your specific option's benefits.

PMB rules apply to South African schemes (Medical Schemes Act). General guide — always confirm with your scheme. Council for Medical Schemes: 0861 123 267.

🛡️ What is Gap Cover?
Show ▼

Gap cover is extra insurance that pays the difference when a specialist, surgeon, or anaesthetist charges more than your medical aid pays. Example: your medical aid pays 100% of the scheme rate, but the surgeon charges 300% — gap cover pays the missing 200%.

⚠️ Most useful if: you have a hospital plan or are planning surgery. Specialist bills of 2–5× the scheme rate are common in private hospitals.

Good to know:

• It is separate from your medical aid — you must already belong to a medical aid scheme.
• Typically costs around R200–R500 per family per month.
• Waiting periods may apply (often 3–12 months for existing conditions).
• It does NOT cover day-to-day costs like GP visits or medicine.

✈️ Travel & Medical Insurance Abroad
Show ▼

If you get ill or injured outside South Africa, your medical aid's normal benefits do NOT apply overseas — you need travel cover. A hospital stay abroad can cost millions of rand.

💡 First check what you already have:
• Many SA schemes (Discovery, Bonitas, Momentum etc.) include emergency international travel cover for trips up to ±90 days — but you must often register/activate it BEFORE you leave. Phone your scheme.
• If you paid for your ticket with a credit card, the card often includes basic travel insurance automatically — check with your bank.

When to buy extra travel insurance:

• Trips longer than your scheme's cover period
• Adventure activities (diving, skiing, hiking)
• Travel to countries with expensive healthcare (USA!)
• Schengen-visa countries require proof of medical travel cover (min €30,000)
• If you have chronic conditions — declare them, otherwise claims may be rejected

📖 Health Conditions Guide

Plain-language info. Visit your nearest clinic — applies to all patients including those without medical aid.

❤️ Hypertension (High Blood Pressure)▼
High blood pressure means your heart works too hard. You usually have NO symptoms — called the "silent killer."

⚠️ Emergency: Severe headache, blurred vision, chest pain → go immediately.

Get BP checked free at any pharmacy. Never stop medication without your doctor.
🍬 Diabetes Type 2▼
Blood sugar builds up and damages the body if not treated.

⚠️ Warning: Very thirsty, urinating a lot, blurred vision, slow-healing wounds, tingling feet.

Never stop medication without your doctor. Wound that won't heal → go to clinic immediately.
💨 Asthma▼
Airways narrow, making it hard to breathe — especially with exercise, cold air, dust, smoke.

🚨 Emergency: Reliever inhaler (blue pump) not helping after 10 minutes → call 10177.
🎗️ HIV / ARTs▼
ARTs (antiretroviral treatment) control the virus. ARTs are FREE at all SA public clinics.

Take every day at the same time. Never miss doses. Undetectable viral load = cannot pass HIV to others (U=U).
🫁 Tuberculosis (TB)▼
TB is curable with 6 months of treatment. Never stop early.

⚠️ Symptoms: Cough 2+ weeks, coughing blood, night sweats, weight loss, fatigue → go to clinic immediately. Testing is FREE.

  2. Tap the Share button ⬆️ at the bottom

  1. Open www.myhealthonestop.com in Chrome
  2. Tap the menu button ⋮ at the top right
  3. Tap "Add to Home screen" (or "Install app")
  4. Tap Add — done!
